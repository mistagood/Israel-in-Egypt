## The 400-Year Prophecy and the Timing of Israel’s Possession of the Land

The fulfillment of the 400-year prophecy given to Abraham in Genesis 15:13–16 aligns not with Israel’s entry into Canaan, but with the moment they secured and possessed the land through conquest. The prophecy specifies a span of four hundred years during which Abraham’s descendants would be sojourners, oppressed, and then brought back to the land in fulfillment of the covenant promise. The terminus of this period is marked by Israel’s possession of Canaan under Joshua, not merely their arrival.

Israel entered Canaan in 1164 BC after the forty-year wilderness period. The major campaigns of conquest followed immediately—Jericho, Ai, the southern coalition, and the northern coalition—over a span of approximately five years. By 1159 BC, the land was effectively subdued, and Israel had achieved control sufficient for tribal allotments to begin. This marks the prophetic moment of possession.

The apportionment of the land commenced around 1158 BC. God’s declaration to Joshua—“Thou art old and stricken in years, and there remaineth yet very much land to be possessed”—signals the administrative transition from warfare to systematic distribution. Caleb’s inheritance at age eighty-five corroborates this moment within the internal chronological structure.

Counting four hundred years back from the point of possession in 1159 BC aligns precisely with the call of Abraham in 1559 BC, producing an exact four-century span that satisfies the covenant’s declared duration. This chronology integrates the patriarchal ages, the 140-year Egyptian sojourn from Jacob’s arrival to the Exodus, and the forty-year wilderness period without contradiction. It clarifies the distinction between entry and possession, demonstrating that the completion of conquest—not mere arrival—marks the terminus of the prophesied four hundred years.
