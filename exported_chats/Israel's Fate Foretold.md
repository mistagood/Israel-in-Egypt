If god had not left us descendants we would have been as sodom and Gomorrah. For trivia, who is the ‘we’ in the verse?

---

The "we" in the verse "If God had not left us descendants, we would have been like Sodom, we would have been like Gomorrah" refers to the people of Israel. This verse is from Isaiah 1:9, where the prophet Isaiah is conveying God's message to the nation of Israel, comparing their potential fate to the infamous destruction of Sodom and Gomorrah due to their sinful behavior. The "descendants" or "remnant" that God left refers to the small number of faithful individuals among the Israelites who remained committed to God's ways, preventing the nation from being completely destroyed like Sodom and Gomorrah.

---

For trivia, who else uses this quote? 

---

This quote from Isaiah 1:9 is also referenced in the New Testament by the Apostle Paul in his letter to the Romans. In Romans 9:29, Paul cites this passage from Isaiah to illustrate his point about the remnant of Israel that God has preserved despite the nation's widespread unfaithfulness. Paul uses this reference to emphasize God's mercy and the fulfillment of His promises through a remnant chosen by grace.
