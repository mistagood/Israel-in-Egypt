What are the parts of the substantia nigra

The **substantia nigra** is a critical structure in the **midbrain** (mesencephalon), and it plays a central role in **movement control**, **dopamine production**, and **reward pathways**. It is divided into two main parts:

---

### 1. **Pars Compacta (SNc)**  
- **Latin:** *Pars compacta* = "compact part"  
- **Function:**  
  - Primary source of **dopaminergic neurons** in the brain.
  - Sends **dopamine projections** to the **striatum** (caudate and putamen) via the **nigrostriatal pathway**, which is essential for **voluntary movement**.
- **Cell Type:** Darkly pigmented **neuromelanin-containing dopaminergic neurons**.
- **Clinical Relevance:**  
  - **Degeneration of SNc neurons** is the hallmark of **Parkinson's disease**, resulting in **dopamine deficiency** in the striatum.

---

### 2. **Pars Reticulata (SNr)**  
- **Latin:** *Pars reticulata* = "net-like part"  
- **Function:**  
  - Primarily composed of **GABAergic inhibitory neurons**.
  - Functions similarly to the **internal segment of the globus pallidus (GPi)**.
  - Acts as an **output nucleus** of the **basal ganglia**, sending inhibitory signals to the **thalamus** and **superior colliculus**.
- **Role:** Helps regulate **eye movements**, **motor planning**, and **habitual behaviors**.

---

### Bonus Subdivision (Less Commonly Used):

- **Pars Lateralis**: Sometimes referenced as a lateral extension of the pars reticulata, but not always classified separately in anatomical texts. Its functional distinction is minimal.

---
