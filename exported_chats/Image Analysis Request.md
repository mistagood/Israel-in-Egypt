## **The Great Stumbling Block: A Scriptural Exposé**

### 1. **Prophetic Warning: Do Not Mourn for the Dead, but for the Exiled**

**Jeremiah 22:10**  
> “Weep ye not for the dead, neither bemoan him: but weep sore for him that goeth away: for he shall return no more, nor see his native country.”

- **Key Point:**  
  The focus is exile, not death or resurrection. Mourning for the exiled prince is the warning, not for the dead king.

---

### 2. **The Prince Who Goes Out: Ezekiel’s Living Parable**

**Ezekiel 12:12**  
> “And the prince that is among them shall bear upon his shoulder in the twilight, and shall go forth… he shall cover his face, that he see not the ground with his eyes.”

- **Key Point:**  
  The true “prince among you” is sent out, quietly, as the scapegoat—not slain in spectacle, not resurrected.

---

### 3. **The Scapegoat Ritual: Not Resurrection, but Removal**

**Leviticus 16:21–22**  
> “And Aaron shall lay both his hands upon the head of the live goat… and shall send him away by the hand of a fit man into the wilderness… and the goat shall bear upon him all their iniquities unto a land not inhabited…”

- **Key Point:**  
  The ritual is not about dying and rising; the atonement is the removal of sin/exile—fulfilled quietly, not with spectacle.

---

### 4. **Idolatry of the Resurrection Doctrine: Weeping for Tammuz**

**Ezekiel 8:14**  
> “Then he brought me to the door of the gate of the LORD’s house… and, behold, there sat women weeping for Tammuz.”

- **Key Point:**  
  Tammuz was the ancient “dying-and-rising god.” The prophets warn: do not mourn for the dead or celebrate resurrection cults—this is idolatry.

---

### 5. **The Stumbling Block: False Prophets and Visions from Their Own Heart**

**Ezekiel 13:2–3**  
> “Son of man, prophesy against the prophets of Israel… that prophesy out of their own hearts… Woe unto the foolish prophets, that follow their own spirit, and have seen nothing!”

**Jeremiah 23:16–17**  
> “Hearken not unto the words of the prophets that prophesy unto you: they make you vain: they speak a vision of their own heart, and not out of the mouth of the LORD.”

- **Key Point:**  
  False prophets—Paul being the most prominent—promote visions and doctrines not found in Torah or the prophets. This creates a stumbling block.

---

### 6. **Paul Admits to Deceit and Craftiness**

**2 Corinthians 12:16**  
> “But be it so, I did not burden you: nevertheless, being crafty, I caught you with guile.”

**Romans 3:7**  
> “For if the truth of God hath more abounded through my lie unto his glory; why yet am I also judged as a sinner?”

**1 Corinthians 9:19–22**  
> “I am made all things to all men, that I might by all means save some.”

**2 Corinthians 11:13**  
> “For such are false apostles, deceitful workers, transforming themselves into the apostles of Christ.”

- **Key Point:**  
  Paul admits to using craft, trickery, and lies for his cause. He boasts of his ability to deceive (“with guile”) and to become “all things to all men”—adapting his message for effect, not truth.

---

### 7. **Benjamin: The Son of the Right Hand and the Son of Sorrow**

**Genesis 35:18**  
> “And it came to pass, as her soul was in departing… she called his name Ben-oni [son of my sorrow]: but his father called him Benjamin [son of the right hand].”

- **Key Point:**  
  Benjamin’s identity is twofold: “son of sorrow” and “son of the right hand.”  
  Paul, the Benjamite (Philippians 3:5), embodies both the “man of sorrow” (perdition) and the instrument (right hand) God uses for retribution.

---

### 8. **The Work of the Right Hand: Man-Made Salvation and Idolatry**

**Isaiah 44:17–20**  
> “And the residue thereof he maketh a god, even his graven image: he falleth down unto it, and worshippeth it… He feedeth on ashes: a deceived heart hath turned him aside… is there not a lie in my right hand?”

- **Key Point:**  
  The “work of the right hand” is crafting a salvation/idol with human effort. Paul’s doctrine (“believe in Christ, not Torah”) is this man-made salvation.

---

### 9. **Paul: The Tent-Maker, Building a New House**

**Acts 18:3**  
> “And because he was of the same craft, he abode with them, and wrought: for by their occupation they were tentmakers.”

- **Key Point:**  
  Paul literally and figuratively builds “tents” (temporary dwellings), a symbol of spiritual exile and instability. Israel is led into “dwelling in tents” again—back into captivity.

---

### 10. **God’s Retribution: Blindness, Exile, and the Man of Lawlessness**

**Romans 9:22**  
> “What if God, willing to show his wrath, and to make his power known, endured with much longsuffering the vessels of wrath fitted to destruction…”

**2 Thessalonians 2:3**  
> “Let no man deceive you by any means: for that day shall not come, except there come a falling away first, and that man of sin be revealed, the son of perdition…”

- **Key Point:**  
  Paul even admits his role is to bring about Israel’s stumbling (Romans 9) and hints at the revelation of the “man of lawlessness” (the “son of perdition”/Benjamin).

---
