The Three Signs of Moses are recorded in the book of Exodus (Exodus 4:1-9). God instructs Moses to perform these signs before the Israelites to demonstrate His divine presence and authority. Here's a brief overview of the signs:

1. **The Staff Turned into a Serpent**: Moses' staff becomes a serpent when he throws it on the ground, and then it turns back into a staff when he picks it up by the tail.
2. **The Leprous Hand**: Moses puts his hand inside his cloak, and when he pulls it out, it is leprous, like snow. He puts it back, and it is restored.
3. **Water Turned to Blood**: Moses is told that he can pour water from the Nile onto dry ground, and it will become blood.

These signs serve several functions in the biblical text, including authenticating Moses' divine mission, demonstrating God's power over creation, and symbolizing aspects of Israel's condition and God's deliverance.

### A New Interpretation

Considering your interests in African American studies, theology, and cultural anthropology, here's a new interpretation that could align with those themes:

#### 1. **The Staff/Serpent: Transformation and Authority**
- **New Interpretation**: The transformation between a staff and a serpent could symbolize the duality of power and oppression. The staff, a symbol of authority, becomes a serpent, often associated with danger or deceit.
- **Application to African American Experience**: This could parallel the transformation of power structures, such as how leadership or authority can be both empowering and oppressive.

#### 2. **The Leprous Hand: Marginalization and Restoration**
- **New Interpretation**: The leprous hand symbolizes marginalization and "otherness." Leprosy in the Bible often signifies exclusion and impurity. The restoration of the hand could symbolize redemption and inclusion.
- **Application to African American Experience**: This might represent the historical marginalization of African Americans and the ongoing struggle for recognition, dignity, and equality.

#### 3. **Water to Blood: Life, Suffering, and Renewal**
- **New Interpretation**: Water, essential for life, turns into blood, a symbol of both life and suffering. This transformation could signify a profound change in the condition or status of something vital.
- **Application to African American Experience**: This could be seen as an allegory for the African American experience itself – a journey through suffering towards renewal, with blood symbolizing both the pain of oppression and the vitality of community and culture.

### Conclusion

This new interpretation of the Three Signs of Moses creates a rich tapestry of symbolism that can be applied to a contemporary context, particularly in the realm of African American studies and liberation theology. It connects ancient symbols with modern struggles and aspirations, providing a fresh lens through which to understand both the biblical text and contemporary human experience. As with any theological or cultural interpretation, this reading would need to be handled with care and nuance, recognizing the diversity of perspectives within both the biblical tradition and the African American community.
