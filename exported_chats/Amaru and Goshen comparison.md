## Amarna (Akhetaten) and Goshen — Are They the Same Region?

The city **Akhenaten built** is **Akhetaten**, known today as **Amarna**. The Egyptian name means **“Horizon of the Aten.”** It was a purpose-built capital created during his religious revolution.

Your question is whether **Amaru**—a variant pronunciation sometimes used in fringe or alternative histories—could be **that city**, and whether it lies **close to Goshen**, the region where the Hebrews lived.

To answer it cleanly, we compare **biblical geography** with **Egyptian historical geography**.

---

## 1. **Location of Akhetaten (Amarna)**
Archaeology places Akhenaten’s capital **in Middle Egypt**:

- Roughly **200 miles (320 km) south of Cairo**  
- On the **east bank of the Nile**  
- Opposite modern Mallawi  
- Between Hermopolis (Ashmunein) and Asyut  

This is **deep in Middle Egypt**, not the Delta.

This means **Amarna is not near Goshen**.

---

## 2. **Location of Goshen**
The biblical region of **Goshen** is consistently pointed toward the **eastern Nile Delta**:

- Mentioned as near **Rameses** (Genesis 47:11)  
- The “best of the land,” associated with **pastoral territory**  
- Located where Semitic groups historically settled (Delta/Hyksos region)  

The Delta is **northern Egypt**, near the Mediterranean entry.

---

## 3. **Distance Between Them**
- Goshen → Eastern Nile Delta  
- Amarna → Middle Egypt  
- The two regions are **at least 300–350 km apart**

There is **no textual, archaeological, or geographic overlap** between Goshen and the site of Akhenaten’s city.

---

## 4. Why the Names Get Confused  
There are three reasons people sometimes connect “Amaru / Amarna” with Goshen:

### A. **Phonetics**
“Amarna” has a soft-vowel form that can resemble *Amaru*, especially in older transcriptions.

### B. **Joseph–Akhenaten theories**
Since many propose Joseph = Akhenaten or Joseph as a co-regent, people try to place **Joseph’s family (Goshen)** near **Akhenaten’s capital**. But the physical locations don’t align.

### C. **Asenath theory**
If Asenath was an Israelite (through Dinah), then Joseph’s household would be tied to **both**:
- the **Delta region** (her people)
- and **Amarna** (the capital Joseph may have influenced)

But this is a *political* and *dynastic* overlap, not a *geographic* one.

---

## 5. What the Evidence Actually Supports

### **Akhetaten (Amarna)**  
Built by Akhenaten, located in Middle Egypt — **not in or near the Delta**.

### **Goshen**  
Located in the Delta — **far north**.

### **Conclusion**  
**Amaru/Amarna is not the city in Goshen, nor close to it.**  
If Joseph and Akhenaten overlap — through co-regency, marriage (Asenath), or political reform — then Joseph’s own **administrative life** would move between the **Delta** and **Amarna**, but the **city itself is not in the Goshen region**.
