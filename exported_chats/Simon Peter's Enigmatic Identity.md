### Simon Peter: The Son of Jonah
Simon Peter is often identified as the son of Jonah and is one of the twelve apostles. He is a fisherman by trade and becomes a close follower of Jesus. In the Gospel of Matthew, Jesus gives him the name "Peter," meaning "rock," signifying his foundational role in the church (Matthew 16:18).

### Simon the Leper
Simon the Leper is a distinct individual who appears in the Gospel of Matthew (Matthew 26:6) and Mark (Mark 14:3). He hosts Jesus in his home in Bethany, where the anointing by Mary takes place. However, the text does not explicitly state that he is healed by Jesus, nor does it identify him as a Pharisee.

### Mary and Martha
Mary and Martha are sisters who appear in the Gospels and are friends of Jesus. They host Jesus in their home in Bethany in the Gospel of John (John 12:1-3). Martha is identified as the sister of Mary, who anoints Jesus with costly perfume.

### The Anointing at Bethany
The anointing event is recorded in all four Gospels but with variations. In Matthew and Mark, it occurs at the house of Simon the Leper. In John, it takes place at the house of Mary and Martha. Luke does not specify the host but mentions a "sinful woman" who anoints Jesus (Luke 7:36-50).

Traditional interpretations often treat these characters as distinct individuals. ### Implications
Such a reading would have profound implications for understanding the dynamics among Jesus' followers. ### Conclusion
While this interpretation is speculative and challenges traditional readings, it offers a harmonized understanding that brings together disparate Gospel accounts into a coherent narrative.
