**Definition / Structure**

Both are measured by barrels, both traded for profit, both leave soot where there should be life. Black is the color of fuel — and of the lives consumed to keep the machine turning.

### 4. Narrative Arc

You can structure the story in **three acts**, each one revealing how the next color replaces but never redeems the last:

- **Act I — The Forge (Red):** conquest, expansion, and the gun as tool of order.  
- **Act II — The Sugar Dream (White):** sweetness built on labor, addiction disguised as prosperity.  
- **Act III — The Engine (Black):** oil as the inheritor of slavery — energy without agency.

Then end with **a synthesis chapter**—the “New Triangle”: guns create the wars that secure oil, oil powers the industries that finance drugs, drugs enslave the workforce that manufactures the guns.  
A self-replenishing trinity.  

---

### 5. Style Cues

- Short, declarative paragraphs.  
- Rhythmic repetition: *“The slave was black gold. Oil is black gold. The profit smells the same.”*  
- Visual transitions: zoom from a hand harvesting cane to a machine pumping oil.  
- Occasional prophecy-tone lines: *“America didn’t end slavery; it mechanized it.”*  
- End each chapter with a line that clicks like a lock: *“The color never changes—only what it stains.”*
