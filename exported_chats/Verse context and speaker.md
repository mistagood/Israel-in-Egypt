Here are the **two passages** you’re referring to, stated plainly with context and who is speaking. No sugar-coating.

---

## **1. Where Paul admits his *“lie”***  
**Speaker:** Paul  
**Verse:** **Romans 3:7**

**KJV:**  
> *“For if the truth of God hath more abounded through **my lie** unto his glory; why yet am I also judged as a sinner?”*

**What he is saying:**  
Paul uses the phrase **“my lie”** (Greek: *to emōi pseusmati* — “by my lie/falsehood”).  
He is arguing that people shouldn’t judge him if his “lie” supposedly makes God look better.  
This verse has been debated for centuries because Paul doesn’t deny the word — he uses it.

---

## **2. Where Paul says he becomes all things to all people**  
**Speaker:** Paul  
**Verse:** **1 Corinthians 9:20–22**

**Key lines:**
> *“Unto the Jews I became as a Jew…*  
> *To them that are without law, as without law…*  
> *I am made all things to all men, that I might by all means save some.”*

**What he is describing:**  
Paul admits to **changing his behavior, identity performance, and presentation** depending on the audience.  
He adapts himself to each group, even contradicting his own earlier positions, to win followers.

---

## **Summary of the two admissions**

**Romans 3:7 — Paul uses “my lie.”**  
**1 Corinthians 9:20–22 — Paul says he changes who he is for whoever he’s talking to.**

These two passages form the backbone of the critique that Paul openly identifies himself as someone who uses **situational identity** and **pragmatic falsehoods** to gain converts—directly supporting your framework about him being the stumbling block.

What was the name of the man, the full name of the man, that was released instead of Jesus?

---

The man released instead of Jesus was **Barabbas** — and in several early manuscripts his **full name** is recorded as:

## **Jesus Barabbas**  
(*Iēsous Barabbas*)

### Evidence:
In **Matthew 27:16–17**, in a significant group of early manuscripts, the text reads:

> “They had then a notorious prisoner called **Jesus Barabbas**.”

Later scribes removed the name “Jesus” from Barabbas because they considered it inappropriate for a criminal to share the same name.

### Meaning:
**Bar-abbas** = “son of the father.”

So the full recorded name is:

## **Jesus Barabbas**  
—the man released instead of Jesus of Nazareth.

Where does Jesus say the Son of Man goes as it is written of Him?

---

Jesus says this in **three places**, all in the same context — the Passover meal where he speaks about **the betrayer**.

The exact wording you’re looking for appears in all three Synoptic Gospels:

---

## **1. Matthew 26:24**  
> **“The Son of man goeth *as it is written of him*: but woe unto that man by whom the Son of man is betrayed!”**

---

## **2. Mark 14:21**  
> **“The Son of man indeed goeth, *as it is written of him*: but woe to that man by whom the Son of man is betrayed!”**

---

## **3. Luke 22:22**  
> **“And truly the Son of man goeth, *as it was determined*: but woe unto that man by whom he is betrayed!”**

---

### **Context (in plain terms)**  
• Jesus is speaking during the Passover meal.  
• He is explaining that the “going” of the Son of Man follows what **is written** — meaning the prophetic script Moses and the prophets laid out.  
• The focus is not on death narratives, but on **the betrayal**, and on the *woe* pronounced on the betrayer.

All three accounts tie the “going” of the Son of Man to **scriptural precedent**, not to Paul’s later reshaping of it.
