Yes.  
Some translations of Genesis 46:27 and Exodus 1:5 (and the parallel in Acts 7:14) say that **72** descendants of Jacob came into Egypt, while others say **70**.

**Why the difference?**

- **Masoretic Text (Hebrew Bible)** ‚Äì Says **70** persons of Jacob‚Äôs household went down to Egypt. Most translations based directly on the Hebrew, such as the KJV, NASB, and ESV, follow this.
- **Septuagint (Greek OT)** ‚Äì Lists **75** in Genesis 46:27 (by including grandchildren of Joseph born in Egypt). Stephen in Acts 7:14, quoting the Greek tradition, says **75**.
- **Some manuscripts** ‚Äì Have **72** instead of 70 or 75. This number comes from certain Greek and Samaritan textual traditions that appear to harmonize the count by including some but not all of the extra descendants listed in the Septuagint. Latin Vulgate manuscripts sometimes reflect this variant, and a few modern Bibles (like the New Jerusalem Bible in footnotes) mention **72** as an alternate reading.

In short:
- **70** ‚Üí Hebrew Masoretic Text (most OT translations)
- **75** ‚Üí Septuagint & Acts 7:14
- **72** ‚Üí Some Greek/Samaritan/Latin variants

## **3. The 72 Count ‚Äì Some Samaritan Pentateuch & Latin Traditions**

**Example tradition** (Jerome notes this in some Latin Vulgate variants):
- Add **Er** & **Onan** ‚Üí 70 + 2 = 72.

This ‚Äú72‚Äù doesn‚Äôt add *new* Egyptian-born people like the 75 does; it‚Äôs an accounting change rather than a genealogy expansion.
