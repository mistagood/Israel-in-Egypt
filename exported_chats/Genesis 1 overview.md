Genesis 1 is the great overture of Scripture—a structured revelation of order, distinction, and purpose emerging from chaos. It’s not merely a tale of beginnings, but a template for how divine thought manifests as creation.

The chapter opens with profound simplicity: *“In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth.”* The Hebrew begins, **Bereshit bara Elohim**, literally “In beginning, created Elohim.”
