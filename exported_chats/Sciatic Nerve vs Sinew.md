The term "sinew" traditionally refers to a tendon, which is a tough, fibrous connective tissue that connects muscles to bones. The sciatic nerve, on the other hand, is a major nerve in the human body, not a tendon. It is the longest and largest nerve, running from the lower back down through the hips, buttocks, and legs. 

Although the sciatic nerve is not a sinew in the traditional sense, it is often included in discussions of musculoskeletal anatomy due to its location and involvement in leg movement and sensation. In some older texts or translations, the word "sinew" might be used more broadly to refer to tendons, ligaments, or even nerves, which could lead to some confusion. However, in modern anatomical terminology, a sinew specifically refers to tendons, not nerves.
