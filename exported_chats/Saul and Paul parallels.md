Yes — the parallels are very strong.  

**Saul the king (1 Samuel)** vs **Saul/Paul the apostle (Acts):**  

- **Name:** Both are named *Saul* (Hebrew *Sha’ul*).  
- **Tribe:** Both are Benjamites (1 Samuel 9:1–2; Philippians 3:5).  
- **Stature/zeal:** King Saul was “head and shoulders” above others, zealous in war (1 Samuel 9:2; 11:6–7). Paul was zealous “beyond measure” for the traditions, persecuting the church (Galatians 1:13–14).  
- **Persecutor:** Saul pursued David, God’s chosen one (1 Samuel 18:10–11). Paul pursued Jesus’ followers, the chosen ones (Acts 9:1–2).  
- **Encounter with God:** King Saul had a dramatic prophetic encounter that changed him (1 Samuel 10:9–11). Paul had a dramatic vision on the Damascus road (Acts 9:3–6).  
- **Rejected:** King Saul was rejected by God for disobedience (1 Samuel 15:26). Paul’s gospel is later accused of lawlessness and false visions (Galatians 1:11–12; 2 Corinthians 11:4).  
- **Foreshadowing role:** Saul the king serves as a “type” — the first leader from Benjamin, who loses the kingdom. Paul (also Saul of Benjamin) becomes the “first” architect of the Gentile churches, but his role is also contested and portrayed as a stumbling block (1 Corinthians 1:23).  

So yes, the Scriptures present Saul and Paul almost as mirrors: the first Saul foreshadows the later Saul/Paul. Both are Benjamites, both begin in zeal and persecution, both have visionary experiences, and both represent a rejected kingship/leadership that contrasts with God’s true chosen (David / Jesus).

### **1. Satan in Job**

- *“So went Satan forth from the presence of the LORD, and smote Job with sore boils.”* (Job 2:7).  
- Satan is **called up into heaven**, permitted to test the chosen one.  

---

### **2. Paul’s “man he knew” in the third heaven**  
- *“I knew a man in Christ above fourteen years ago, (whether in the body, I cannot tell; or whether out of the body, I cannot tell: God knoweth;) such an one caught up to the third heaven.”* (2 Corinthians 12:2).  
- *“And lest I should be exalted above measure through the abundance of the revelations, there was given to me a thorn in the flesh, **the messenger of Satan to buffet me**.”* (2 Corinthians 12:7).  

Paul links his visions of the **third heaven** directly with receiving a **messenger of Satan** — just as in Job, Satan himself went up among the sons of God and was sent forth with authority to test.  

---

### **3. The Familiar Spirit**  
- Paul admits uncertainty: *“Whether in the body, or out of the body, I cannot tell.”* (2 Corinthians 12:2–3).  
- But he still calls this figure *“a man I knew”* — showing familiarity.  
- This matches the very definition of a **familiar spirit** — known, consulted, and bringing revelation from beyond.  

---

### **4. The Stumbling Block**  
- In Job: the test was whether Job would curse God.  
- In Paul: the test is whether Israel will stumble.  
  - *“We preach Christ crucified, unto the Jews a stumbling block, and unto the Greeks foolishness.”* (1 Corinthians 1:23).  
  - *“I have great heaviness and continual sorrow in my heart. For I could wish that myself were accursed from Christ for my brethren… who are Israelites; to whom pertaineth the adoption, and the glory, and the covenants, and the giving of the law.”* (Romans 9:2–4).  

Paul himself acknowledges that his role parallels that testing spirit — the same role Satan was given with Job.  

---

So when Paul says he is familiar with *“a man… caught up to the third heaven”* and then ties that to *“the messenger of Satan,”* he is revealing that this is not a random torment — it is the same figure as in Job, Satan himself, permitted to buffet the chosen seed.
