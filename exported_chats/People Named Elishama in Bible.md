# People Named Elishama in Bible



---

Who are the people in the Bible named Elishama? 

---



---

There are several people named **Elishama** in the Bible. The name means *“God has heard”* or *“God hears”* (from **El** = God, **shama** = hear). These individuals are mostly mentioned in genealogies and historical records, often tied to royal or tribal leadership. Here's a breakdown of the notable figures:

---

### 1. **Elishama son of Ammihud**  
- **Tribe**: Ephraim  
- **Role**: **Chief/Leader** of the tribe of Ephraim during the wilderness period under Moses  
- **Mentions**:
  - **Numbers 1:10** – Chosen as one of the tribal leaders for the census
  - **Numbers 2:18–20** – Camped on the west side of the tabernacle; prince of Ephraim
  - **Numbers 7:48, 7:53** – Gave offerings during the dedication of the altar
  - **Numbers 10:22** – Led Ephraim's division in the march
- **Importance**: As a direct representative of the house of Joseph, this Elishama links back to prophetic expectations for Ephraimite leadership.

---

### 2. **Elishama, son of David**  
- **Tribe**: Judah  
- **Role**: One of the sons born to **King David** in Jerusalem  
- **Mentions**:
  - **2 Samuel 5:16**, **1 Chronicles 3:8**, **1 Chronicles 14:7**  
- **Note**: Not much is said beyond the listing, but being David's son, he was of royal blood.

---

### 3. **Elishama the scribe**  
- **Role**: A royal scribe in the court of **King Jehoiakim of Judah**  
- **Mentions**:
  - **Jeremiah 36:12, 36:20–21** – His chamber was where Baruch read Jeremiah’s scroll; involved in relaying the scroll to the king
- **Importance**: As a scribe, he was part of the bureaucratic structure during a turbulent prophetic time, linking him to the fate of Jeremiah’s scroll.

---

### 4. **Elishama in genealogy of Benjamin (possibly a different one)**  
- **Mentions**:
  - **1 Chronicles 8:12** – A descendant of Benjamin through the line of Elpaal
- **Note**: A lesser-known Elishama, likely of a different lineage than David’s or Ephraim’s.

---

### Summary of Types:
- **Ephraimite Prince**: Tribal leader during the Exodus
- **Royal Son**: One of David’s many children
- **Scribe**: Involved with Jeremiah’s writings
- **Benjamite**: A genealogical figure
