Compare the four gospels to see how John was killed do they all say how he was killed or even mention it? List the verses from each Gospel that speaks about John’s verse death

---

The death of John the Baptist is indeed mentioned in the New Testament. However, not all the Gospels provide a detailed account of the circumstances surrounding his death. Let's take a look at what each of the four Gospels says about the death of John the Baptist:

1. **Matthew**: 
   - In Matthew 14:1-12, Herod the tetrarch hears reports about Jesus and believes that John the Baptist has been raised from the dead. This prompts a flashback to the circumstances of John's arrest and execution. Herod had arrested John for speaking out against his illicit marriage to Herodias. Herodias's daughter danced for Herod, pleasing him, and he promised her anything she wanted. Prompted by her mother, she asked for John the Baptist's head on a platter. Herod reluctantly agreed, and John was beheaded.
   
2. **Mark**: 
   - Mark 6:14-29 also recounts the story of John's death. Like Matthew, Mark describes Herod's belief that John had been raised from the dead. The account in Mark is quite similar to Matthew's account, detailing the events leading up to John's execution at the behest of Herodias's daughter.

3. **Luke**: 
   - Luke mentions John's imprisonment in Luke 3:19-20 due to his rebuke of Herod because of Herodias and other evil deeds, but the Gospel does not provide a detailed account of John's death.

4. **John**: 
   - The Gospel of John does not provide a narrative of John the Baptist's death.

In summary, only the Gospels of Matthew and Mark provide a detailed narrative of John the Baptist's death. Luke briefly touches on the reason for his imprisonment, and the Gospel of John does not discuss his death.

What did John the Baptist wear?

---

John the Baptist's attire is described in the Gospel of Matthew:

**Matthew 3:4 (ESV)**:
"John wore clothing made of camel's hair with a leather belt around his waist, and his food was locusts and wild honey."

This distinctive attire of camel's hair and a leather belt is symbolic and reflects the prophetic tradition of the Old Testament. For instance, the prophet Elijah is described in a similar manner in the book of 2 Kings:

**2 Kings 1:8 (ESV)**:
"They answered him, 'He wore a garment of hair, with a belt of leather about his waist.' And he said, 'It is Elijah the Tishbite.'"

John's clothing and diet emphasized his ascetic lifestyle, highlighting his role as a voice crying out in the wilderness and preparing the way for the coming of the Messiah. It also links him to the prophetic tradition of the Old Testament, particularly to figures like Elijah.

Where does Jesus go when he hears John has been taken to prison

---

When Jesus heard that John the Baptist had been taken to prison, He withdrew to Galilee. The Gospel of Matthew provides this account:

**Matthew 4:12 (ESV)**:
"Now when he [Jesus] heard that John had been arrested, he withdrew into Galilee."

Following this, Jesus left Nazareth and went to live in Capernaum, which is by the sea, in the territory of Zebulun and Naphtali. This move fulfilled what was spoken by the prophet Isaiah:

**Matthew 4:13-16 (ESV)**:
"And leaving Nazareth he went and lived in Capernaum by the sea, in the territory of Zebulun and Naphtali, so that what was spoken by the prophet Isaiah might be fulfilled: 'The land of Zebulun and the land of Naphtali, the way of the sea, beyond the Jordan, Galilee of the Gentiles— the people dwelling in darkness have seen a great light, and for those dwelling in the region and shadow of death, on them a light has dawned.'"

After settling in Capernaum, Jesus began His public ministry, preaching about the Kingdom of Heaven and calling people to repentance.

Is this the only book that says it?

---

No, the Gospel of Mark also mentions Jesus' movement to Galilee after John the Baptist was arrested:

**Mark 1:14 (ESV)**:
"Now after John was arrested, Jesus came into Galilee, proclaiming the gospel of God,"

In Mark's account, after John's arrest, Jesus begins His ministry in Galilee and starts proclaiming the good news.

However, the Gospels of Luke and John do not directly link Jesus' move to Galilee with John the Baptist's imprisonment in the same way that Matthew and Mark do. They narrate the events in a slightly different sequence or focus on different aspects of Jesus' early ministry.
