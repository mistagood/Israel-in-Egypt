---

### 1. **Chess Terminology**
In chess, a gambit is an **opening strategy** where a player **sacrifices a pawn or other piece** early in the game to gain a stronger position.  
**Example:**  
- The **King's Gambit**: White sacrifices the f-pawn to accelerate development and control the center.

### 2. **General Strategy / Politics / Warfare**

**Examples:**  
- A political leader makes a controversial announcement to shift public opinion (a media gambit).
- A military commander exposes a small force to lure the enemy into a trap.

**Key Traits:**
- **Sacrifice or risk** is deliberate.
- Aimed at gaining **initiative**, control, or a **psychological edge**.

---

### 3. **Conversation and Rhetoric**

**Example:**

---
