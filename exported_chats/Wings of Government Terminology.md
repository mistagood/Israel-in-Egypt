Eagles and vultures belong to the same order, **Accipitriformes**, but they are **not closely related** beyond that. Their differences in behavior, diet, and even anatomy make them distinct despite their superficial similarities.

### **Taxonomic Differences:**
- **Eagles** belong to the **family Accipitridae**, which includes hawks, kites, and buzzards.
- **Old World vultures** (such as the griffon vulture, the biblical *nesher*) also belong to **Accipitridae**, making them **distant cousins of eagles**.
- **New World vultures** (such as the turkey vulture and Andean condor) belong to a completely different family, **Cathartidae**, and are actually **more closely related to storks than to eagles**.

### **Key Differences:**
| Feature | Eagles | Vultures |
|---------|--------|---------|
| **Diet** | Hunt live prey | Scavengers, eat carrion |
| **Beak** | Sharp, curved for tearing flesh | Hooked, adapted for scavenging |
| **Feet/Talons** | Strong, powerful grip for catching prey | Weaker, not designed for gripping prey |
| **Head** | Fully feathered | Often bald to stay clean while eating carcasses |
| **Flight** | Active hunters, flap wings often | Soar for long periods, riding thermal currents |
