Elishama 

---



---

Elishama is a name found in the Hebrew Bible, and it means "God hears" or "God has heard." In biblical contexts, Elishama is associated with a few notable figures:

1. **Elishama, Son of Ammihud**: He was a leader of the tribe of Ephraim during the time of Moses. Elishama is mentioned in the Book of Numbers as one of the leaders who helped organize the Israelites during their journey through the wilderness.

2. **Elishama, Son of Sheshur**: Another figure with the same name, who served as a leader among the Israelites.

In both cases, the name reflects a theme of divine attention and support, aligning with the broader biblical narrative of God's guidance and care for His people.

Ammihud 

---

Ammihud is a name found in the Hebrew Bible, meaning "my people are noble" or "my people are esteemed." In the Bible, Ammihud is primarily known for his role as the father of Elishama, a leader of the tribe of Ephraim. He is mentioned in the Book of Numbers as a prominent figure during the Israelites' journey through the wilderness:

- **Ammihud**: He was a member of the tribe of Ephraim and the father of Elishama, one of the tribal leaders chosen to assist Moses in organizing and leading the Israelites. Ammihud's name is also listed among the heads of the tribes in various genealogical records in the Bible.

Ammihud's inclusion in these records highlights his family's significance and leadership within the Israelite community during the Exodus.
