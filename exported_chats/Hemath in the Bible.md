**Hemath is not a person.**  
**Hemath (also Hamath)** is a **geographical location**—a **northern border city/region** of Canaan/Syria frequently used in boundary formulas of Israel’s promised territory.

There is **no biblical figure named Hemath**.  
There is **only the city/land of Hamath**, which appears repeatedly as a territorial marker.

---

# **1. BASIC DEFINITION**

**Hebrew:** חֵמָת  
**Pronunciation:** Ḥēmāth  
**Meaning:** “Fortress,” “citadel,” or possibly “hot springs” (root *ḥmm*, “to be hot”).  
**Nature:** **City/kingdom** in northern Syria.

Often paired with **Riblah**, **Zobah**, **Arpad**, and **Hamath-Zobah**.

---

# **2. HEMATH AS A BORDER OF ISRAEL**

### **Numbers 34:8**
Sets the **northern boundary** of the promised land:
> “...from Mount Hor you shall mark out your border to the entrance of **Hamath**…”

### **Joshua 13:5**
Lists territory still unconquered:
> “...the land of the Gebalites, and all Lebanon, toward the sunrise, from Baal-gad below Mount Hermon to the entrance of **Hamath**.”

### **1 Kings 8:65**
Defines Solomon’s dominion:
> “...from the entrance of **Hamath** unto the river of Egypt.”

In every case **Hamath marks the far northern extent of the land**.

---

# **3. HEMATH AS A SYRIAN KINGDOM**

### **Genesis 10:18**
Hamath appears indirectly in the **Table of Nations**:
> “...the Hamathite.”  
This means: **Hamath is a descendant city-state of Canaan**, not a person named Hemath.

### **2 Kings 18:34**, **2 Kings 19:13**, **Isaiah 10:9**, **Jeremiah 49:23**
These verses speak of:
- The **king of Hamath**
- Hamath’s downfall by the Assyrians

This confirms Hamath as a recognized **Syrian kingdom**.

---

# **4. THE HOUSE OF HAMATH / KINGS OF HAMATH**

While there is no person named Hemath, the Bible references rulers:

### **2 Kings 17:30**
A specific deity of Hamath is named:
> “The men of **Hamath made Ashima**.”

This shows **Hamath had its own ethnic population and god**, but still no individual named Hemath.

# **5. HEMATH IN DAVID’S DAYS**
