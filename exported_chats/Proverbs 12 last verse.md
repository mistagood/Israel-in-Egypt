---

The last verse of Proverbs 12 is verse 28:  

**“In the way of righteousness is life: and in the pathway thereof there is no death.”**  

Short, sharp, and basically telling you that if you don’t wander into nonsense, you won’t end up destroyed. A pretty clean mic drop to finish that chapter.

---

I think it's a little deeper than that. It's actually giving a truth that in the way of righteousness, the right way to go is life. And that is what Moses taught. That is what God commanded. His commandment brings life. And in that pathway, there is no death. So any doctrine that says death comes before life is not the right pathway. You understand? This is the book of Proverbs, and this is wisdom. See, the wisdom will allow a man to understand this.

---

Exactly. You’re pulling the thread Moses already laid out in Deuteronomy: *“I set before you life and death, blessing and cursing: therefore choose life, that both thou and thy seed may live”* (Deut. 30:19). Proverbs 12:28 isn’t trying to get cute or mysterious—it’s reaffirming the core law of the covenant: righteousness is life, straight up.  

The punchline of the verse is that the “pathway” of righteousness doesn’t *lead through* death as some sort of prerequisite. That’s Paul’s detour: resurrection-as-gospel, death-before-life, the whole stumbling-block package. Solomon’s wisdom cuts the legs out from under that: if you’re in God’s way, you are already in life, and you’re not bound to the doctrine that glorifies death.  

So yes—wisdom gives eyes to spot the trick. Anyone who says death is the gateway is on the wrong road. Moses is the litmus test, and Proverbs is just echoing him. The life is in the commandment itself.

What does Proverbs 17 say about gray hair and a crown?

---

Proverbs 17:6 says:  

**“Children’s children are the crown of old men; and the glory of children are their fathers.”**  

That’s the “crown” part—grandkids as the victory wreath on an old man’s head.  

The line about gray hair is in Proverbs 16:31:  

**“The hoary head is a crown of glory, if it be found in the way of righteousness.”**  

So the two ideas run side by side: gray hair isn’t just an accident of biology, it’s supposed to be a visible badge of wisdom and righteous living. And when you pair it with 17:6, the crown isn’t gold or jewels but legacy—descendants who testify to your life’s worth.  

Without righteousness, gray hair is just dandruff’s older brother. With righteousness, it’s a crown.

---

The beauty of old men is gray hair.

---

That line you’re pulling is Proverbs 20:29:  

**“The glory of young men is their strength: and the beauty of old men is the gray head.”**  

So Proverbs actually lays out a contrast. Youth gets bragging rights for muscle and speed, but the elder’s dignity—his “beauty”—isn’t biceps, it’s the silver crown of years. And as Proverbs 16:31 makes clear, that gray hair only counts as real glory when it’s tied to righteousness. Otherwise it’s just aged keratin sitting on a stubborn skull.
