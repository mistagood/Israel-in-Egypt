Leviticus 16 is a central chapter in understanding the biblical concept of atonement, particularly as it relates to the Day of Atonement (Yom Kippur). Here's how you can structure your class notes, slides, and charts.

### 1. Introduction to Leviticus 16
- **Context**: The chapter outlines the specific rituals Aaron, the high priest, must perform on the Day of Atonement. This is a sacred day when the entire community of Israel seeks atonement for their sins.
- **Purpose**: To emphasize the seriousness of sin, the holiness of God, and the necessity of purification for the community.

### 2. Structure of Leviticus 16
Divide the chapter into key sections:
- **Verses 1-2**: Instructions to Aaron about entering the Holy of Holies.
- **Verses 3-10**: Preparations for the Day of Atonement.
- **Verses 11-14**: The sin offering for Aaron and his household.
- **Verses 15-19**: The sin offering for the people.
- **Verses 20-22**: The scapegoat ritual.
- **Verses 23-28**: Concluding rituals and purification.
- **Verses 29-34**: Institution of the Day of Atonement as a perpetual statute.

### 3. Detailed Breakdown

#### **Verses 1-2: Approach to the Holy of Holies**
- **Slide Content**:
  - *Warning to Aaron*: He must not enter the Holy of Holies at just any time.
  - *Reason*: God's presence is manifested in a cloud over the mercy seat.
  - *Implication*: The holiness of God requires careful and specific actions.

#### **Verses 3-10: Preparations**
- **Slide Content**:
  - *Offerings*: Aaron must bring a bull for his own sin offering and a ram for a burnt offering.
  - *Two Goats*: One is for the Lord (sin offering), and the other is the scapegoat (Azazel).
  - *Chart*: Visual representation of the offerings and their purposes.

#### **Verses 11-14: Atonement for the High Priest**
- **Slide Content**:
  - *Purification of Aaron*: Aaron first offers the bull for himself and his household.
  - *Blood Sprinkling*: Blood is sprinkled on the mercy seat to cleanse it.
  - *Chart*: Steps in the purification process for the High Priest.

#### **Verses 15-19: Atonement for the People**
- **Slide Content**:
  - *Sin Offering*: The goat designated for the Lord is sacrificed.
  - *Blood Ritual*: The same blood sprinkling ritual is performed for the people.
  - *Chart*: Compare and contrast the atonement process for the priest and the people.

#### **Verses 20-22: The Scapegoat Ritual**
- **Slide Content**:
  - *Confession Over the Goat*: Aaron lays hands on the scapegoat, confessing all Israel's sins.
  - *Sent Away*: The goat is sent into the wilderness, symbolizing the removal of sin.
  - *Symbolism*: The scapegoat represents the complete removal of sin from the community.

#### **Verses 23-28: Final Rituals and Cleansing**
- **Slide Content**:
  - *Cleansing Rituals*: Aaron removes his linen garments, bathes, and dresses in regular garments.
  - *Burnt Offerings*: Final offerings are made to conclude the ritual.
  - *Purification*: Those involved in handling the offerings must purify themselves.

#### **Verses 29-34: Perpetual Statute**
- **Slide Content**:
  - *Annual Observance*: The Day of Atonement is to be observed annually on the 10th day of the 7th month.
  - *Sabbath Rest*: The people are to afflict their souls and observe a Sabbath rest.
  - *Chart*: Calendar of Israel's sacred feasts with emphasis on Yom Kippur.

- **Comparison Chart**: Contrast between the two goats (one for the Lord, one for Azazel).
- **Calendar Chart**: Placement of Yom Kippur in the Jewish calendar.

  - *Ongoing Relevance*: How the Day of Atonement informs Christian views on sin, repentance, and forgiveness.
