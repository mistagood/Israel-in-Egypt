The distinction between Purgatory and Hell is an interesting aspect of Christian theology. In traditional Christian doctrine, Purgatory and Hell serve different purposes and occur at different times in the eschatological timeline.

Purgatory is often understood, particularly in Catholic theology, as a temporary state of purification for souls who have died in a state of grace but still need to be cleansed of residual sin before entering Heaven. It's not a place of punishment but rather a purifying process, allowing souls to be fully sanctified.

Hell, on the other hand, is described in the Bible as a place of eternal punishment for those who are ultimately condemned. In the Book of Revelation, Hell is often equated with the "lake of fire," which is depicted as the final destination for Satan, his followers, and those who are not found in the Book of Life at the end of the world. This suggests that Hell, as the lake of fire, is a future reality that comes into existence after the final judgment.
