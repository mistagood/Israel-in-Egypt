Creating a power of attorney (POA) involves legal documentation that grants you and your sisters the authority to make decisions on behalf of your mother. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you through the process:

### Steps to Create a Power of Attorney

1. **Understand the Types of POA**:
   - **General Power of Attorney**: Broad powers to act on behalf of your mother.
   - **Limited (Special) Power of Attorney**: Specific powers for certain tasks or for a limited period.
   - **Durable Power of Attorney**: Remains effective if your mother becomes incapacitated.
   - **Medical Power of Attorney**: Specific to healthcare decisions.

2. **Discuss with Your Mother**:
   - Ensure your mother understands the implications and consents to granting you and your sisters the POA.
   - Decide the type of POA that best fits her needs.

3. **Draft the Document**:
   - **Online Templates**: Use reputable sources like LegalZoom, Rocket Lawyer, or your state’s bar association.
   - **Lawyer Assistance**: For a customized and legally sound document, consider hiring an attorney specializing in elder law or estate planning.

4. **Include Essential Information**:
   - **Principal**: Your mother’s full name and address.
   - **Agents**: Your and your sisters' full names and addresses.
   - **Powers Granted**: Clearly specify what powers are granted to the agents.
   - **Durability**: State if the POA is durable.
   - **Effective Date**: When the POA becomes effective.

5. **Notarization and Witnesses**:
   - Most states require the POA to be notarized. Check your state’s requirements.
   - Some states also require witnesses. Ensure you have the necessary number of witnesses who are not related to the principal or agents.

6. **Sign the Document**:
   - Your mother must sign the document in the presence of a notary and any required witnesses.
   - The agents may also need to sign, acknowledging their responsibilities.

7. **Distribute Copies**:
   - Provide copies to all relevant parties, including financial institutions, healthcare providers, and anyone else who may need to honor the POA.

8. **Store Safely**:
   - Keep the original document in a secure place, such as a safe.
   - Ensure all agents know where the document is stored and how to access it if needed.

### Example of a Power of Attorney Document

```plaintext
POWER OF ATTORNEY

I, [Mother’s Full Name], residing at [Mother’s Address], hereby appoint my daughters, [Your Full Name], [Sister 1’s Full Name], and [Sister 2’s Full Name], residing at [Your Addresses], as my attorneys-in-fact to act on my behalf in any and all matters, effective immediately.

This Power of Attorney shall be durable and remain in effect even in the event of my incapacitation.

Powers Granted:
1. Manage all my financial affairs, including banking, investments, and real estate transactions.
2. Make healthcare decisions on my behalf, including consenting to or refusing medical treatment.
3. Handle all legal matters, including litigation and settlement negotiations.

I revoke any prior Powers of Attorney granted by me.

Signed this [day] of [month], [year].

_________________________
[Mother’s Signature]

Notarized by:
_________________________
[Notary Public’s Signature and Seal]

Witnessed by:
1. _________________________
[Witness 1’s Signature]
2. _________________________
[Witness 2’s Signature]
```

### Tips

- **Consult an Attorney**: While online templates are convenient, an attorney can ensure the document meets all legal requirements and addresses your specific situation.
- **State Laws**: POA laws vary by state, so ensure compliance with your state’s regulations.
- **Communication**: Keep open communication with your mother and sisters about the responsibilities and expectations involved in holding a POA.

By following these steps, you can create a legally sound power of attorney that empowers you and your sisters to make decisions on behalf of your mother when needed.
