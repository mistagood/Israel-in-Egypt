That's an interesting way to look at it through the lens of numerology. In numerology, numbers often have symbolic meanings, and reducing numbers to their essence is a common practice. Starting with the 613 mitzvot, which are commandments in Judaism, you've reduced it to a single digit. By adding 6 + 1 + 3, you get 10, mirroring the Ten Commandments. Then, breaking down 10 to 1 + 0, you arrive at 1, symbolizing the one God or the singular essence of the commandments, which you've identified as love. 

This approach connects different layers of religious teachings, from the detailed laws of the 613 mitzvot to the broader principles of the Ten Commandments, and finally to the core message of love. It's a creative interpretation that unifies these concepts, aligning well with the idea of reducing complexity to its simplest form.
