In the Quran, Jesus (known as 'Isa) is a significant prophet who holds a revered position, but his role differs markedly from that in Christian tradition. Here’s a detailed look at Jesus’ role in the Quran and the concept of the other messenger:

### Jesus in the Quran

1. **Prophet and Messenger**: Jesus is described as a prophet and a messenger sent by God. He is revered for his miracles and his role in conveying God’s message to the Children of Israel. Key miracles attributed to Jesus in the Quran include healing the sick, raising the dead, and creating a bird from clay (Quran 3:49).

2. **The Crucifixion**: Contrary to the New Testament, the Quran asserts that Jesus was not crucified. Instead, it teaches that Jesus was not killed or crucified but was raised up to heaven by God. The Quran states that it only appeared as if Jesus had been crucified to those who witnessed it (Quran 4:157-158). This denial of crucifixion implies a significant departure from Christian beliefs about Jesus’ sacrifice and resurrection.

3. **Second Coming**: The Quran prophesies that Jesus will return before the Day of Judgment. His return is expected to coincide with the final confrontation between good and evil, during which he will restore justice and defeat the false messiah (al-Masih ad-Dajjal) (Quran 43:61).

4. **Role in Judgment**: Jesus will testify against the people on the Day of Judgment about their adherence to God’s commandments and his own prophetic mission. His role is that of a witness and a clarifier of God’s truth (Quran 5:116-117).

### The Other Messenger

The Quran does not specify a distinct "other messenger" who accompanies Jesus, but several traditions within Islamic eschatology and Hadith literature describe the return of Jesus alongside other significant figures. These include:

1. **The Mahdi**: In many Islamic traditions, the Mahdi is a messianic figure who is expected to appear before the end of the world to restore justice and righteousness. The Mahdi’s role is often seen as complementary to Jesus’ second coming. Jesus and the Mahdi are believed to work together to defeat the forces of falsehood and establish a just order (Hadith collections such as those by Sahih Muslim and Sahih Bukhari).

2. **The Role of Angels**: Islamic eschatology also mentions the involvement of angels in the events surrounding the end times. They play roles in supporting the Mahdi and Jesus in their final confrontation against evil forces.

### Synthesis

In summary, Jesus in the Quran is a revered prophet whose role diverges from Christian beliefs about his death and resurrection. His expected return in Islamic eschatology signifies a crucial role in the end times, working alongside other key figures like the Mahdi to restore justice and confront evil. The differences in these narratives reflect broader theological divergences between Islam and Christianity, while also providing a rich tapestry of religious expectations and roles in the final days.
