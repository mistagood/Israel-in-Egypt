What's Solomon, King Solomon's other name?

---



---

King Solomon is also known by the name **Jedidiah**. This name, meaning "beloved of the Lord," was given to him by the prophet Nathan at the command of God, as recorded in **2 Samuel 12:24-25**. The name Jedidiah reflects the special favor and love that God had for Solomon. However, throughout most of the Bible, he is primarily referred to by his more well-known name, Solomon.

---

Why is he called Solomon 

---

Solomon's name, which means "peace" or "peaceful" in Hebrew, is tied to the circumstances of his birth and reign. His father, King David, had fought many wars during his reign, but Solomon's rule was marked by a period of peace and stability for Israel. God promised David that his son would inherit the throne and that his kingdom would experience peace (1 Chronicles 22:9). This peaceful reign was part of God's covenant with David, and the name Solomon symbolizes the fulfillment of that promise.

The name "Solomon" also reflects the hope that his reign would bring rest and prosperity to the nation, allowing him to focus on building the Temple in Jerusalem, a task that his father David had wanted to complete but was prevented from doing due to the wars he fought.

I'm not asking about the meaning, I'm asking about why we call him Solomon and where it is said that his name is given in the Bible.

---

King Solomon is called "Solomon" in the Bible because that was the name his father, King David, gave him at birth. The first mention of his name is in **2 Samuel 12:24**, where it says, "Then David comforted his wife Bathsheba, and he went to her and lay with her. She gave birth to a son, and they named him Solomon."

Additionally, in the very next verse, **2 Samuel 12:25**, it says that the prophet Nathan, sent by God, gave him the name **Jedidiah** (meaning "beloved of the Lord") as a divine name. But despite this, the name Solomon is the one predominantly used throughout the biblical narrative, and it became his official name as king. 

So, while God gave him the name Jedidiah, the name "Solomon" is the one that became most widely recognized and used, particularly as a reflection of his peaceful reign and his role as David's successor.
