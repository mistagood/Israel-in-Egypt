1. **Grizzled** (Hebrew: *ברדים* – *beruddim*)

   - Think of something not solid-colored but marbled — a mix of light and dark patches.  
   - Appearance: grey, salt-and-pepper, dappled.  
   - So “grizzled horses” would not be pure white, pure black, or pure red — but **a patterned mixture**.  

2. **Bay** (Hebrew: *אמשים* – *’amutzim*)  
   - Root idea: strong, vigorous, reddish-brown.  
   - In horse terms, a *bay* is a **reddish coat with black points** (mane, tail, lower legs).  
   - Appearance: a dominant reddish-brown body, not patchy like “grizzled,” but a **solid blend** of red + dark tones.
