Additionally, the testicular arteries, which supply blood to the testicles, arise from the abdominal aorta, the main artery in the abdomen. This vascular connection also ties the testicles to the abdominal region.

Why is the stomach called the second brain?

---



---

The stomach, or more broadly the gut, is often referred to as the "second brain" due to the complex and extensive network of neurons found in the enteric nervous system (ENS). This system is capable of functioning independently of the brain and spinal cord and is responsible for regulating many digestive processes. Here are a few reasons why the gut is called the "second brain":

1. **Extensive Neural Network:** The enteric nervous system contains approximately 100 million neurons, more than the spinal cord, enabling it to control gut behavior independently of the brain.

2. **Neurotransmitter Production:** The gut produces many of the same neurotransmitters that are found in the brain, such as serotonin, dopamine, and gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA). In fact, about 90% of the body's serotonin is produced in the gut, which influences mood and emotional well-being.

3. **Gut-Brain Axis:** The gut and brain are connected through the vagus nerve and other biochemical signaling pathways, forming the gut-brain axis. This connection allows for bidirectional communication, influencing both digestive health and mental states.

4. **Impact on Mood and Behavior:** The gut microbiome, consisting of trillions of bacteria, also plays a role in the gut-brain connection. Changes in the gut microbiota can affect mood and cognitive function, potentially contributing to conditions like anxiety and depression.

5. **Autonomy in Functioning:** The enteric nervous system can regulate many digestive processes independently, such as peristalsis (the movement of food through the digestive tract) and enzyme secretion, without input from the central nervous system.

These features highlight the significant role the gut plays in not only digestion but also in overall physical and mental health, leading to its nickname as the "second brain."

- **מַצֵּבָה (Matzevah):** The word emphasizes the concept of standing or erecting something as a lasting reminder, reflecting permanence and dedication.
