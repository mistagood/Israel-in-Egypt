> “The land of Zebulun, and the land of Naphtali… by the way of the sea, beyond Jordan, Galilee of the nations.  
> The people who sat in darkness saw a great light…”

Matthew’s narrative logic is clear:  
Jesus leaves Nazareth and **moves into the boundary-region of Zebulun** specifically in order to fulfill the prophecy.

## **2. The cities involved:**

Matthew gives the three-point movement:

1. **Nazareth** (ancestral home but *not* in Zebulun; Nazareth is in Naphtali’s territory)

3. **“Sea coast”** (Sea of Galilee shoreline)

So Matthew’s wording—**“in the borders”**—is deliberate. Not deep inside Zebulun, but the boundary region where Zebulun meets Naphtali and the sea-route (“Via Maris”).

This is the only explicit textual statement tying Jesus to Zebulun’s territory.

## **3. Are there *other* Zebulun locations Jesus is tied to?**

No New Testament writer names any other city in Zebulun by name.

Zebulun’s historical cities (Old Testament list: e.g., Jokneam, Bethlehem of Zebulun, Chisloth-tabor, Idalah, Beth-lehem, etc.) **never appear in the Jesus narratives.**

The only Zebulun reference is the Isaiah/Matthew prophecy and the geographic description of Capernaum’s location.
