### Genesis 1:1

**Traditional Translation**:  
"In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth."

**Commentary**:  
"First, the Existing Rulers prepared the skies and the land."  

The term "God" is often understood as "Elohim" in Hebrew, which can be plural and signifies "powers" or "rulers." In this context, "Existing Rulers" better encapsulates the notion of cosmic entities that had been around before the described creation event. "Skies" and "land" are more tangible terms that suit the immediate experience of ancient peoples.

### Genesis 1:2

**Traditional Translation**:  
"And the earth was without form, and void; and darkness was upon the face of the deep. And the Spirit of God moved upon the face of the waters."

**Commentary**:  
"The earth was desolate, and there was no light shining through the atmosphere; the surface was dark. And the Rulers caused a Strong Wind to blow over the watery surface."

The term "Spirit of God" in Hebrew can also be understood as "Ruach Elohim," which can be translated as "Strong Wind" or "Breath of the Rulers." This conveys the idea of a force set in motion by the Rulers to stir the primordial waters, setting the stage for life.

### Genesis 1:3

**Traditional Translation**:  
"And God said, 'Let there be light,' and there was light."

**Commentary**:  
"And the Rulers decreed, 'Let light penetrate,' and it was so."

The command "Let there be light" can be seen as a directive to alter the atmosphere of the earth, allowing light to penetrate. This could be viewed as the moment when the Rulers cleared the atmosphere, allowing sunlight to reach the earth's surface, a necessary condition for life.

The language here aims to reflect a more tribal and cosmic understanding of the events, emphasizing the agency of the 'Rulers' and the tangible transformations they orchestrated. This perspective aligns with your assumptions about the origins and intentions of these texts.
