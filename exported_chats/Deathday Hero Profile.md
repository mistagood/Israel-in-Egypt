### Superhero Trading Card: Deathday

---

**Name**: **Deathday**

**Alias**: **The Clockkeeper**  
 
**Alignment**: **Neutral Good**  

---

**Powers**:  
- **Death Vision**: Deathday has the uncanny ability to see the exact time and manner of anyone’s death simply by looking at them. This vision is vivid and undeniable, like watching a scene from the future unfold in real-time.  

**Strengths**:  
- **Forewarning**: Can use his power to save lives by warning others of imminent danger or helping them make peace with their destiny.  
- **Moral Compass**: Despite his grim knowledge, Deathday remains dedicated to ensuring others find hope and closure.  
- **Detective Insight**: Often collaborates with authorities to stop crimes before they happen, using his knowledge of impending tragedies.  

**Weaknesses**:  
- **Burden of Knowledge**: Constantly haunted by the inevitability of death, especially for loved ones.  
- **Fate Bound**: Deathday cannot change the *when* of someone’s death, only the *how*.  
- **Social Isolation**: Struggles to form deep connections due to his grim gift.  

---

**Appearance**:  
- **Eyes**: Glowing silver when using his power.  
- **Costume**: A sleek black suit with streaks of white resembling cracks in a clock face. The suit’s emblem is an hourglass with sand frozen in time.  
- **Height**: 6’1”  
- **Build**: Lean but imposing.  

---

**Backstory**:  
Born with his powers, Deathday spent much of his childhood tormented by visions of people's deaths, including his parents' tragic demise. Over time, he learned to accept his abilities, turning them into a force for good. Now, he roams the world as both a protector and a guide, helping people confront their mortality and make their time count.  

---

**Catchphrase**:  
*"Every moment matters when the clock is ticking."*  

---

**Stats**:  
- **Intelligence**: 9/10  
- **Strength**: 6/10  
- **Speed**: 7/10  
- **Durability**: 5/10  
- **Power**: 10/10  
- **Charisma**: 8/10  

---

**Collectible Quote**:  
*"I don’t see death as an end. I see it as the final page of your story—make it a good one."*  

--- 

**First Appearance**: *The Clock Strikes You #1 (2024)*
