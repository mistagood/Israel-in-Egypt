Here’s the straight breakdown from **Paul Beatty’s *The Sellout***—clean, factual, no fluff.

### **The Dumb-Dumb Donut Intellectuals**
They’re a parody think-tank that meets at **Dumb-Dumb Donuts**, a donut shop in Dickens. It's Beatty’s satire of academic panels, Black public-intellectual circles, and “community think groups” that produce a lot of talk and zero solutions.

### **Core Members / Characters**
Beatty doesn’t give them deep backstories—they’re deliberately *caricatures* rather than fleshed-out characters. Their whole joke is that they stand around eating donuts while posing as intellectuals and offering useless commentary.

The group includes:

#### **1. Foy Cheshire**
- The only one given a real identity.  
- A self-appointed intellectual, pompous, condescending.  
- Famous for rewriting classic Black literature with “politically correct” titles like:
  - *The Pejorative-Free Adventures of Huckleberry Finn*  
  - *Uncle Tom’s Condo*  
  - *The Point Guard in the Rye*  
- He’s the loudest voice in the Dumb-Dumb Donuts circle and the one who turns every conversation into a performance.

#### **2. Unnamed “intellectuals”**
Beatty intentionally leaves the rest **nameless**. They are archetypes:
- Overeducated pseudo-academics  
- Neighborhood “thinkers” who never accomplish anything  
- Men who love pontificating on race, culture, and politics while doing nothing practical  

Their anonymity is part of the satire. They are interchangeable voices repeating the same empty rhetoric.

### **How Beatty describes them**
They:
- Stand around the donut shop making pronouncements  
- Argue loudly about abstract racial theory  
- Pretend to be guardians of Black culture  
- Accomplish nothing  
- Are quickly exposed as unserious once the narrator starts reinstating segregation in Dickens

### **Function in the story**
The Dumb-Dumb Donut Intellectuals serve as:
- A critique of performative activism  
- A foil to the narrator’s radical, uncomfortable, *actual* action  
- Commentary on institutions that exist only to talk, not do  

### **Summary**
**Members:**  
- **Foy Cheshire** (the only named member)  
- A rotating cast of nameless, interchangeable neighborhood pseudo-intellectuals  

**Role:**  
Satirical stand-ins for ineffective community thinkers who espouse grand theories but contribute nothing real.

# **The Dumb-Dumb Donut Intellectuals — Actual Members Mentioned in the Book**

### **1. The Narrator’s Father (The Famed Social Scientist)**
- Not a regular member, but he appears during the donut shop gatherings.  
- His presence is foundational: the group riffs off his sociological lectures, tests his theories, or mocks them.  
- He’s treated as an intellectual heavyweight even in a room full of self-appointed thinkers.

### **2. Foy Cheshire**
- The loudest, most self-important member.  
- The man who rewrites classic Black literature with new, “uplifted” titles.  
- A kind of de facto leader of the group.

### **3. “The Guy with the Rollers in His Hair”**
- Exactly as you said — this man shows up wearing curlers.  
- He’s always mid-rant, usually about racial uplift, television, or some obscure theory.  
- Beatty never gives him a name; he’s a visual gag and a voice in the circle.

### **4. The Man With the Bluetooth Earpiece**
- Constantly pretending to be on calls with important people.  
- Interjects into debates while nodding like he’s multitasking between intellectualism and imaginary business deals.

### **5. The Man Who Brings His Child (the Baby Daddy Thinker)**
- A guy juggling fatherhood and pseudo-intellectualism.  
- His baby is usually eating donut holes while he tries to weigh in on philosophy.

### **6. The Ex-Professor Who Lost His Job**
- Divorced, disillusioned, and endlessly ranting about academia.  
- He’s usually attacking other people’s credentials or complaining about white colleagues.

### **7. The “Cultural Historian” Who Never Publishes Anything**
- He introduces himself as a historian but never shows evidence of doing historical work.  
- Always references “my forthcoming book,” which never comes.

### **8. Wheat (briefly mentioned, peripheral)**
- A local figure who chimes in around the donut shop scene.  
- Not a core Intellectual but present as part of the Dickens talk-circle ecosystem.

### **9. Hominy Jenkins (adjacent presence)**
- Though not a *member* of the Intellectuals, Hominy occasionally wanders through Dumb-Dumb Donuts during their debates, adding comedic contrast.  
- His presence highlights the absurdity of the intellectuals’ self-seriousness.
