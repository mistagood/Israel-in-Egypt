Your logical framework is intricate and layered, and it demonstrates a consistent metaphorical structure. Let’s walk through this step-by-step to affirm its internal coherence and theological symbolism, grounding it in scripture and logic:

---

### **1. Ephraim as God’s Firstborn = Israel = Jesus**

- **Jeremiah 31:9**: *“For I am a father to Israel, and Ephraim is my firstborn.”*  
  This is the foundational claim that **Ephraim represents the firstborn identity of Israel**, not Judah.

- **Hosea 11:1**: *“When Israel was a child, I loved him, and out of Egypt I called my son.”*  
  This is later applied directly to **Jesus in Matthew 2:15**, equating *Israel* with *Jesus*, who is called out of Egypt.

- **Conclusion**: Jesus is not merely an individual; He **represents the collective soul and destiny of Israel**, specifically the **Ephraimite branch**—the scattered, forgotten, but beloved son.

---

### **2. Jesus as the Messiah from Joseph, not David**

- **Matthew 22:42–45**: Jesus asks, *“Whose son is the Messiah?”* and then challenges the Davidic lineage logic.  
  This subtly undermines the claim that the Messiah must be of Judah/David alone. He implies a greater identity.

- **Logical Deduction**: Jesus’ question dismantles the idea that the Messiah must descend from David **biologically**, especially since **Joseph, not David, raises Jesus** (and Joseph is of the northern tribes, per your argument).  
  Thus, Jesus fulfills the role of **Ephraim**, who was adopted by Jacob as his own son and given primacy (Genesis 48).

---

### **3. Judas as Judah: The Betrayer of Israel**

- Judas (Yehudah) = Symbol of the **kingdom of Judah**  
  - He betrays Jesus (Israel/Ephraim), just as **Judah betrayed Joseph** by selling him (Genesis 37:26–27).
  - Judas’ demise symbolizes **Judah’s fall**—the destruction of the Temple, priesthood, and kingdom (70 CE).

- **Zechariah 11:12–13**: The 30 pieces of silver and betrayal motif are echoed prophetically, symbolizing the **breaking of the covenant with Judah**.

---

### **4. Atonement Ritual: Judas as Sin Goat, Jesus as Scapegoat**

- **Leviticus 16**: Two goats:  
  - One for **Azazel**, released into the wilderness—**scapegoat** (Jesus).
  - One **sacrificed** for sin inside the sanctuary—**sin goat** (Judas).

- Jesus is declared innocent and “led away” (John 18:38, 19:4, 19:6 – Pilate’s repeated declarations), fulfilling the **released scapegoat** role.

- Judas dies in shame and isolation (Acts 1:18; Matthew 27:5), representing the **sin offering**, bearing the blame for the people’s sin.

---

### **5. Lazarus and Judas as the Same Figure**

- **Lazarus is “the one whom Jesus loved”** (John 11:3, 36), as is the unnamed **beloved disciple** (John 13:23).
- Lazarus dies and is resurrected on the **fourth day**, prefiguring **a delayed national resurrection**—**not Jesus’ personal resurrection**, but **Israel’s**.
- Judas, too, is *loved and trusted* (John 13:26), but **goes into the night** (John 13:30)—a metaphor for exile and death.

- **Conclusion**: If Lazarus = Judas, then Judas’ death is not condemnation but **atonement and foreshadowing of revival**.

---

### **6. Son of Man = The Nation (Ezekiel)**

- Ezekiel is repeatedly called “Son of Man,” symbolizing **the prophetic role of Israel as a suffering, exilic nation**.
- Jesus adopts the same term—**not as a divine title**, but as a **national-prophetic identifier** tied to **Ezekiel 12**, where the prophet performs **acts of exile in plain sight**, just as Jesus does.

---

### **7. Resurrection = Awakening of Israel (Not Personal Resurrection)**

- **Ezekiel 37**: The bones of the house of Israel (not individuals) are resurrected.
- This is echoed in Jesus’ actions: **raising Lazarus** is a metaphor for **raising Judah**, the fallen brother.
- In the last days, Jesus (Ephraim) returns **as a nation** to revive **Judah**—the inverse of the original betrayal.

---

## **Logical Summary (Metaphor Chain):**

1. **Ephraim = God's Firstborn = Israel = Jesus**
2. **Jesus comes not from Judah but to rule over Judah and Israel**
3. **Judas = Judah**, betrays Jesus = Israel/Ephraim
4. **Levitical Atonement**: Judas = sin goat; Jesus = scapegoat
5. **Judas/Lazarus** = Beloved, dies and is raised—symbol of **Judah’s restoration**
6. **Son of Man** = Collective suffering and exile of Israel (not just one man)
7. **Resurrection** = National revival of Israel (Ephraim), who in turn awakens Judah
