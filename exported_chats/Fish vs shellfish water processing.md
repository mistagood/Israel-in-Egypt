## ðŸŸ Fish with Fins and Scales  
**(e.g., salmon, trout, cod)**

### 1. **Water Filtration (Osmoregulation)**
- **Gills**: Water flows in through the mouth, over the gills, and out the operculum. Gills extract oxygen and expel carbon dioxide.
- **Salt and Water Balance**:
  - In **freshwater**, fish absorb water passively and excrete excess through **dilute urine**.
  - In **saltwater**, fish **drink seawater**, extract water, and actively expel excess salt through specialized **chloride cells** in the gills.

### 2. **Waste Excretion**
- **Feces**:
  - Processed in the intestines and excreted through the **anus**.
  - Solid fecal matter is generally compact and expelled away from the body.
- **Urine**:
  - Excreted through the **urinary pore**, filtered by **kidneys**.
  - Contains nitrogenous waste (primarily **ammonia** in freshwater fish, **urea** or **trimethylamine oxide (TMAO)** in marine fish).
- **Scales**:
  - Provide a **semi-permeable barrier**, minimizing passive diffusion of toxins or waste from the water into the body.
  - Also reduce microbial colonization and external contamination.

**Key Point**: Water passes through the gills for gas exchange, and waste is excreted in **separate organs**, not back through the same intake system.

## ðŸ¦ Shellfish  
**(e.g., clams, oysters, mussels, shrimp, crabs, lobsters)**

### 1. **Water Filtration**
- **Filter Feeders** (e.g., oysters, clams, mussels):
  - Draw in **large volumes of water** through **siphons** or gill-like structures.
  - Trap food particles (plankton, organic matter), but also **bacteria, viruses, heavy metals, and fecal particles**.

- **Scavengers** (e.g., crabs, lobsters, shrimp):
  - Not true filter feeders, but consume **decaying organic matter**, feces, and carcasses on the seafloor.
  - Their environment exposes them to **sediment-borne toxins** and **detritus** with high microbial content.

### 2. **Waste Processing & Excretion**

  - Expelled through the **hindgut or rectum**, but not well-separated from the food path.
  - Some shellfish (e.g., bivalves) produce **pseudofeces**â€”particles rejected before digestionâ€”that are expelled but may still linger in the **mantle cavity**.
- **Urine/Ammonia**:
  - Nitrogenous waste (mostly **ammonia**) is excreted **directly through body surfaces or gill membranes**, not via a complex kidney system.
  - Ammonia can **diffuse both in and out**, depending on concentration in the water.
  - Some have rudimentary **antennal or green glands** for waste excretion, but these are not highly efficient.

- Many contaminants such as:
