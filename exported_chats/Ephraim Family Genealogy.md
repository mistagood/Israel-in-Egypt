The Bible provides a genealogical account of Ephraim, the son of Joseph, primarily in the books of Genesis, Numbers, and 1 Chronicles. Here's a summary of the family members associated with Ephraim as outlined in the Bible:

### **Ephraim's Family Members**
1. **Father**:
   - **Joseph** (Genesis 41:50-52) - The eleventh son of Jacob and Rachel, who became a ruler in Egypt.

2. **Mother**:
   - **Asenath** (Genesis 41:50) - Daughter of Potiphera, priest of On (Heliopolis).

3. **Brother**:
   - **Manasseh** (Genesis 41:51) - Ephraim's older brother.

4. **Sons** (Ephraim’s descendants):
   - **Shuthelah** (Numbers 26:35; 1 Chronicles 7:20) - The firstborn of Ephraim.
   - **Beker** (1 Chronicles 7:20) - A son of Ephraim.
   - **Tahan** (1 Chronicles 7:20) - A son of Ephraim.
   - **Ezer** (1 Chronicles 7:21) - Mentioned as Ephraim’s descendant.
   - **Elead** (1 Chronicles 7:21) - Mentioned as Ephraim’s descendant.

5. **Grandchildren** (Descendants of Ephraim through Shuthelah):
   - **Eran** (Numbers 26:36) - Son of Shuthelah.
   - Other unnamed descendants (1 Chronicles 7:21-27).

6. **Great-grandchild** (Famous in lineage):
   - **Joshua (Hoshea)** (1 Chronicles 7:27; Numbers 13:8) - Son of Nun, from the tribe of Ephraim, who led Israel into the Promised Land.

### Key Notes:
- Ephraim's family experienced tragedy as recorded in 1 Chronicles 7:20-22, where some of his descendants were killed by men of Gath, leading to Ephraim mourning deeply and later having more descendants.
- Ephraim's descendants formed the tribe of Ephraim, which played a significant role in Israel's history. The tribe often represented the Northern Kingdom of Israel as a whole, indicating its prominence.

Hosea, the prophet to the Northern Kingdom of Israel (which includes the tribe of Ephraim), had several prophetic children whose names carried symbolic meanings relevant to Israel's relationship with God. These children were a part of Hosea's divinely ordained message, reflecting the state of the nation and God’s plan for its future.

---

### **Hosea’s Prophetic Children and Their Symbolism**

#### **1. Jezreel**
- **Hebrew Name**: יִזְרְעֶאל (Yizre'el)
- **Meaning**: "God sows" or "God scatters."
- **Significance**:
  - **Judgment**: The name symbolizes God's coming judgment on the house of Jehu for the bloodshed in the valley of Jezreel (Hosea 1:4-5).
  - **Restoration**: The name also holds a dual prophecy, foretelling the eventual restoration of Israel. As a farmer sows seed, God would scatter His people, but they would later be gathered back to Him.

#### **2. Lo-Ruhamah**
- **Hebrew Name**: לֹא רֻחָמָה (Lo-Ruhamah)
- **Meaning**: "No mercy" or "Not loved."
- **Significance**:
  - **Judgment**: The name represents God's withdrawal of mercy from Israel due to their persistent sins (Hosea 1:6).
  - **Future Redemption**: Despite the judgment, God assures a future time when mercy will be shown to Judah and ultimately to all His people.

#### **3. Lo-Ammi**
- **Hebrew Name**: לֹא עַמִּי (Lo-Ammi)
- **Meaning**: "Not my people."
- **Significance**:
  - **Rejection**: The name reflects the broken covenant relationship between God and Israel (Hosea 1:9). They had abandoned Him, so He declared they were no longer His people.
  - **Restoration**: In Hosea 2:23, the prophecy shifts to hope, where God promises to reclaim His people, saying, "You are my people," and they will respond, "You are my God."

---

### **Symbolism in Hosea’s Family**
Hosea’s children were living prophecies, their names serving as messages to the Northern Kingdom:

1. **Ephraim as the Central Tribe**: Hosea’s connection to the tribe of Ephraim, often symbolic of the Northern Kingdom, amplifies the prophetic significance of his family. Ephraim represents fruitfulness but also spiritual waywardness, as seen in the prophet’s call for repentance and return to God.

2. **A Message of Judgment and Redemption**: The children’s names highlight God’s justice but also His unwavering commitment to restore Israel. This parallels the New Testament themes of chastisement followed by grace and salvation.

3. **Messianic Foreshadowing**: The names also align with broader Messianic prophecies, pointing to the ultimate reconciliation of God and His people through the Messiah, whom Ephraim (fruitfulness) symbolically anticipates.

    > "I will say to those called 'Not my people,' 'You are my people'; and they will say, 'You are my God.'"
  - This reversal symbolizes the restoration of the covenant relationship between God and His people.

- **Symbolic Connection to Ephraim and Israel**:
  - **Ephraim as Representative of Israel**: As the largest and most prominent tribe in the Northern Kingdom, Ephraim often stands as a symbol for all of Israel. God's promise to reclaim His people directly includes Ephraim, whose name ("Fruitful") foretells a time of renewed spiritual growth and prosperity.
  - **Messianic Implications**: The promise of "Ammi" points forward to the ultimate gathering of God's people through the Messiah, where the covenant will be fully restored for all who belong to Him, whether Jew or Gentile (Romans 9:25-26).

- **Hebrew Name**: רֻחָמָה (Ruhamah)
- **Meaning**: "Mercy" or "Loved."
- **Context in Hosea**:
  - The name **Lo-Ruhamah** ("No mercy") in Hosea 1:6 reflects God's temporary withdrawal of mercy due to Israel's unfaithfulness.
  - In Hosea 2:1, the judgment is reversed as the people are once again called:
    > "Say to your brothers, 'Ammi,' and to your sisters, 'Ruhamah.'"
  - This reversal signifies the return of God's steadfast love and mercy toward His people.

- **Symbolic Connection to Ephraim and Israel**:
  - **Ephraim’s Dual Identity**: Ephraim embodies both the rejection (due to idolatry) and the promise of mercy (through repentance). The Northern Kingdom's judgment was severe, but the promise of "Ruhamah" affirms that God's mercy will triumph.

1. **Judgment and Redemption**:

   - This reflects the cycle of **judgment (Lo-Ammi/Lo-Ruhamah)** and **restoration (Ammi/Ruhamah)** throughout biblical history.

2. **Ephraim as a Symbol of God’s Mercy**:
