The conflict between Moses and Paul reveals a deep divide between direct, face-to-face revelation from God and visions that can lead to deception. Yet, Saul's ultimate failure as a king and his rebellion against God illustrate the danger of relying on visions or sudden conversions alone. Like his namesake, Paul also experiences a dramatic transformation on the road to Damascus, but he never attains the certainty of revelation that Moses had.

The Bible warns about the unreliability of visions. God Himself says that if a prophet has a vision and is deceived, it is because He has allowed it (Ezekiel 14:9). Visions, while important, can mislead, and are not as trustworthy as face-to-face communication with God. Paul's teachings, which at times conflict with Moses' law, fall under this category of questionable vision-based prophecy. In this light, Moses’ teachings remain supreme. Paul's visions, like those of other prophets who did not communicate with God face to face, cannot challenge the clarity and certainty of the Law delivered to Moses. Therefore, where Paul's teachings diverge from the Torah, they align with the warning of Deuteronomy 18:22, marking him as a false prophet, failing the test laid out by the very standard Moses himself established.
