Joshua 16:10 (KJV) says:

> *“And they drave not out the Canaanites that dwelt in Gezer: but the Canaanites dwell among the Ephraimites unto this day, and serve under tribute.”*

This verse is profound in its implications.

Ephraim, who was given an inheritance in the land, failed to fully drive out the indigenous population of Canaan. Instead, the Canaanites continued to live *among* them—physically present, culturally enduring, and economically subjugated.

**Spiritually and prophetically, what does this mean for Ephraim?**

It means Ephraim is not a *pure* or isolated people. Ephraim becomes, over time, *mixed with the people*—a recurring indictment found in later prophetic texts (e.g., Hosea 7:8: *“Ephraim, he hath mixed himself among the people; Ephraim is a cake not turned.”*) That’s not just poetic—it’s political. A cake not turned is half-baked: one side burned, the other raw. It’s symbolic of cultural dissonance and dual identity.

Now, if the Canaanites represent African peoples—dark-skinned, indigenous, often identified as Hamitic by historical interpretations—and Ephraim lived among them, then the bloodlines and cultural influences *intertwined*. That’s not incidental—it’s prophetic.

**So what does this say about the Negro in America?**

The American Negro is a *composite identity*—not just one tribe, not just one nation. They are Ephraimites who have, through captivity, slavery, and survival, become *entwined with the Canaanite* (African) and indigenous American populations. They are the descendants of Joseph—cut off from their homeland, *exiled into Egypt again*, as Deuteronomy 28:68 foretold—but also *mixed with the people*, reflecting the very nature of Ephraim's spiritual DNA: powerful, fruitful, yet fragmented.

This is not a weakening—it is the *fulfillment* of prophecy.

Ephraim’s mixture is not a flaw, it’s a sign. It marks the place of awakening. Because in the last days, God will call Ephraim from the nations where he was scattered (Jeremiah 31:20), and the memory of his identity will return.

**Key takeaway:**
The “Negro” in America is not simply African, nor simply slave, but *Ephraimite*, spiritually and genealogically. And just as in Joshua’s day, Ephraim lives among the Canaanites—now under a new kind of tribute: mass incarceration, economic exploitation, and cultural erasure. But the prophecy foretells not just mixture, but *restoration*.
