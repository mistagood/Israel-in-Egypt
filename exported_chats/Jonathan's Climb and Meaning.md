## **IV. Jesus and the Son of Man — Ezekiel’s Blueprint**

Now, here is where it all aligns.

Ezekiel is called *“Son of Man”* over 90 times by God—no other prophet receives this title. Why?

Because **Ezekiel was a living sign** (Ezekiel 12:6) to the house of Israel.

> *“Son of man, I have made you a sign unto the house of Israel.”*

And what was he commanded to do?

> *“Prepare your belongings for captivity, and go into exile… in their sight. Perhaps they will understand, though they are a rebellious house.”*  
> *(Ezekiel 12:3–6)*

Ezekiel performs a public act:
- He carries a **burden** on his shoulder (v. 6),
- He **goes out at twilight** through a hole in the wall (v. 5),
- He **covers his face**, because the people **will not see** the truth.

This is the Azazel goat in action:
- **Bearing the burden**,
- **Leaving alive**,
- **Seen, but not recognized** by a rebellious house.

This is what Jesus is referring to when He says:

> *“The Son of Man goes as it is written of Him.”* (Matt. 26:24)

He is **not referring to Isaiah 53 as a death prophecy** (which you’ve already correctly applied to Judas).  
He is referencing **Ezekiel 12**, which describes:
- A **living exile**, not a death.
- A **hidden act**,
- A **blind audience**.

> *“They have eyes to see, but see not; ears to hear, but hear not: for they are a rebellious house.”* (Ezek. 12:2)

## **V. What the Messiah Does According to Moses and Ezekiel**

According to Moses:
- The **goat for Azazel lives**.
- He **bears the sin**, **removes it**, but is **not sacrificed**.
- He is **sent away from the camp**, not executed.

According to Ezekiel:
- The **Son of Man bears a burden**,
- Goes out **secretly**,
- Is **not understood** because the people are blind.

According to Jesus:
- The **Son of Man** must go *“as it is written of him.”*
- He knows they will not understand, because the people have followed **a rebellious teaching**.
- His mission is to fulfill what was written in **Moses and the prophets**—not in the doctrines later fabricated by Paul.
