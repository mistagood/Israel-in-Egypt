The phrase "I don't want to have to condescend to speak to someone" can be interpreted as dismissive or offensive because it implies a sense of superiority. If you're concerned about how to express the difficulty of simplifying complex ideas for someone without sounding condescending, you could phrase it more thoughtfully. 

For example, you might say, "I find it challenging to communicate these ideas in a way that's easy to understand without oversimplifying them," or "I want to make sure I'm expressing these concepts clearly without losing the depth of their meaning." This approach shows respect for the other person while acknowledging the difficulty of translating complex ideas into simpler terms.
