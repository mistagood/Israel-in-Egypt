# Slavery Abolition Timeline



---

List of when slavery was abolished throughout the world in transatlantic slave trade times

---



---

Here’s a list of when slavery was abolished in various regions of the world during the time of the transatlantic slave trade:

### **18th Century**
- **1772** – Britain (Somersett's Case effectively ended slavery in England, but not in the empire)
- **1793** – Canada (Upper Canada Act Against Slavery, gradually abolished)
- **1794** – France (First abolition during the French Revolution, reinstated by Napoleon in 1802)
- **1799** – New York (Gradual abolition, fully ended in 1827)

### **19th Century**
- **1803** – Denmark-Norway (First country to ban the transatlantic slave trade)
- **1804** – Haiti (First nation founded by former enslaved people)
- **1807** – Britain (Abolition of the transatlantic slave trade, but slavery continued)
- **1808** – United States (Abolition of the transatlantic slave trade, but slavery remained legal)
- **1811** – Spain (Abolished the slave trade, but not slavery itself)
- **1813** – Sweden (Abolished the slave trade)
- **1814** – Netherlands (Abolished the slave trade)
- **1820** – U.S. (Making participation in the slave trade punishable by death)
- **1823** – Chile (Abolished slavery)
- **1829** – Mexico (Abolished slavery)
- **1833** – British Empire (Slavery Abolition Act, took effect in 1834, with full emancipation in 1838)
- **1835** – Portugal (Abolished slave trade north of the equator, full abolition in colonies later)
- **1847** – Sweden (Full abolition of slavery in its territories)
- **1848** – France (Second abolition, after reinstating it in 1802)
- **1851** – Colombia (Abolished slavery)
- **1852** – Argentina (Final abolition of slavery)
- **1854** – Venezuela (Abolished slavery)
- **1863** – Netherlands (Abolition in Suriname and the Dutch Caribbean, but freed people had to work for another decade)
- **1865** – United States (13th Amendment abolished slavery)
- **1869** – Portugal (Final abolition in colonies)
- **1873** – Puerto Rico (Slavery abolished, with conditions)
- **1886** – Cuba (Slavery abolished)
- **1888** – Brazil (Last country in the Americas to abolish slavery, via the Golden Law)

### **20th Century**
- **1906** – China (Ended legal slavery)
- **1923** – Afghanistan (Abolished slavery)
- **1926** – Nepal (Abolished slavery)
- **1928** – Iran (Abolished slavery)
- **1936** – Ethiopia (Abolished slavery under Emperor Haile Selassie)
- **1948** – Universal Declaration of Human Rights (Declared slavery illegal worldwide)
- **1952** – Qatar (Abolished slavery)
- **1962** – Saudi Arabia and Yemen (Final abolition of slavery)
- **1981** – Mauritania (Last country to formally abolish slavery)

Even though formal slavery was abolished, various forms of forced labor and human trafficking persist in many regions today.
