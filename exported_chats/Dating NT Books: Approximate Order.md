The dating of the New Testament books is based on scholarly consensus, and there is some debate about the exact years. Here is a comprehensive list of the New Testament books arranged roughly in the order in which most scholars believe they were written:

1. **James**: 45-50 AD
2. **Galatians**: 48-49 AD
3. **1 Thessalonians**: 50-51 AD
4. **2 Thessalonians**: 51-52 AD
5. **1 Corinthians**: 53-54 AD
6. **2 Corinthians**: 55-56 AD
7. **Romans**: 56-57 AD
8. **Philippians**: 60-62 AD
9. **Colossians**: 60-62 AD
10. **Philemon**: 60-62 AD
11. **Ephesians**: 60-62 AD
12. **Mark**: 65-70 AD
13. **1 Peter**: 64-65 AD
14. **2 Peter**: 65-68 AD
15. **Matthew**: 70-80 AD
16. **Luke**: 70-80 AD
17. **Acts**: 80-85 AD
18. **Hebrews**: 80-90 AD
19. **1 Timothy**: 62-64 AD
20. **Titus**: 62-64 AD
21. **2 Timothy**: 66-67 AD
22. **Jude**: 80-90 AD
23. **John**: 90-100 AD
24. **1 John**: 90-95 AD
25. **2 John**: 90-95 AD
26. **3 John**: 90-95 AD
27. **Revelation**: 95-96 AD

These dates are approximations, and scholarly opinions can vary.
