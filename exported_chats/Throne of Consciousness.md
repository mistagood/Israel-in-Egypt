---

You can absolutely use Photoshop assets in both **After Effects (AE)** and **Blender** for creating 3D objects and effects, but how you approach it depends on the depth of 3D you want and the workflow you prefer.

---

## **1. Using Just After Effects**
After Effects can handle layered Photoshop (.PSD) files directly:
- **Import as Composition** ‚Üí retains layers so you can animate each separately.
- Use **3D Layer Mode** to move layers in 3D space.
- Apply **cameras, lights, shadows, depth of field** for a 2.5D parallax look.
- Add **3D effects plug-ins** like *Element 3D* (Video Copilot) to extrude PSD shapes into actual 3D geometry.

**Pros**:
- Fast setup.
- Great for 2.5D motion graphics and light 3D.
- Stays entirely in AE.

**Cons**:
- Limited to "fake" 3D unless you use plug-ins.
- Not as powerful for complex modeling or realistic rendering.

---

## **2. Using Photoshop Assets in Blender**
Photoshop assets can also be used in Blender for **true 3D modeling**:
- Save layers or elements from Photoshop as PNGs with transparency.
- Import them into Blender as **image planes**.
- Use them as textures for 3D objects (UV mapping).
- Extrude vector paths from Photoshop into 3D meshes (via SVG export).
- Create fully modeled, lit, and textured 3D scenes.
- Render animations and composite the result back into AE.

**Pros**:
- True 3D environment with full modeling and physically based rendering.
- Perfect for objects that need to move or rotate in full 3D space.
- More realistic lighting, shadows, and materials.

**Cons**:
- Longer learning curve.
- Extra step to render and bring footage back to AE.

---

## **3. Hybrid Workflow (Best of Both Worlds)**
Many professionals use:
1. **Photoshop** ‚Üí design and create base art.
2. **Blender** ‚Üí turn flat designs into real 3D models/scenes and render them.
3. **After Effects** ‚Üí composite the 3D renders with motion graphics, typography, and VFX.

This way:
- Blender handles realism and complex 3D movement.
- AE handles compositing, effects, and final polish.

---

If your project only needs flat layers moving in 3D space (like camera moves through a scene), **just AE** might be enough.  
If you want the assets to rotate, extrude, or interact like actual 3D objects, **Photoshop + Blender + AE** is the way to go.
