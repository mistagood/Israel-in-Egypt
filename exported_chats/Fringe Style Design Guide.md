## **II. WORD-BY-WORD BREAKDOWN (from Hebrew)**

Let’s take each term and translate directly:

---

### 1. **Fringes** – *Tzitzit* (צִיצִת)

- Root: *tzitz* — meaning **blossom**, **tassel**, or **projection**.
- Appears in temple and priestly imagery as **something that sprouts or dangles**, like the edge of a garment or crown (*e.g.* Exodus 28:36).
- Function: a visible **marker**, not hidden, calling to mind covenant remembrance.

---

### 2. **Borders** – *Kanaf* (כָּנָף)

- Root meaning: **wing**, **edge**, **corner**, **skirt**.
- Not merely “seam” or “borderline” but a **strategic, outermost point**—used in poetic and prophetic language for protection (e.g. “under the shadow of His wings”).

**This would be interpreted not as “anywhere” but at the garment’s edges—likely four corners.**

---

### 3. **Put Upon the Fringe... a Ribband of Blue**  
**Hebrew**:  
וְנָתְנוּ עַל־צִיצִת הַכָּנָף פְּתִיל תְּכֵלֶת  
**Transliteration**:  
*Venatnu al-tzitzit hakanaph, p'til tekhelet*  
**Translation**:  
“And you shall put **upon the fringe of the corner a *thread (cord)* of blue (tekhelet).”*

---

### 4. **Ribband** – *P'til* (פְּתִיל)

- Root meaning: **twisted cord**, **thread**, **string**, sometimes a fine thread or cord used for holy garments and binding.
- Also used in *Genesis 38* (Tamar and Judah: "cord and signet")—not a ribbon **around** something but a **distinct strand**.

**Conclusion**: "Ribband" = **cord**, **thread**, **strand**, not a modern ribbon or sash.

---

### 5. **Tekhelet** (תְּכֵלֶת) – “Blue”

- Ancient blue dye derived from a sea creature (*murex trunculus* or similar).
- Rich, deep blue—used in the tabernacle, high priest garments, veils.
- Symbolic of **divinity, royalty, covenant**, and **heavenly law**.
