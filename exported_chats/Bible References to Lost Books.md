The Bible contains a few references to other books and writings, some of which are not included in the canonical texts of the Bible but are considered part of other religious traditions or historical writings. Here are some key references where the Bible mentions other books:

### References to Other Books in the Bible

1. **Book of Jasher (Sefer HaYashar):**
   - **Joshua 10:13**: "So the sun stood still, and the moon stopped, till the nation avenged itself on its enemies, as it is written in the Book of Jasher."
   - **2 Samuel 1:18**: "And he ordered that the people of Judah be taught this lament of the bow (it is written in the Book of Jasher)."

   The Book of Jasher, also called the "Book of the Upright," is a lost book referenced in the Old Testament. It is believed to have contained additional historical accounts and poetry not included in the canonical Bible.

2. **Book of the Wars of the Lord:**
   - **Numbers 21:14**: "Therefore it is said in the Book of the Wars of the Lord: Waheb in Suphah and the valleys of the Arnon..."

   This is another lost text mentioned in the Old Testament. The "Book of the Wars of the Lord" likely contained ancient songs or poetic descriptions of Israel's battles and victories.

3. **Book of the Chronicles of the Kings of Israel and Judah:**
   - Throughout **1 Kings** and **2 Kings**, there are multiple references to the "Book of the Chronicles of the Kings of Israel" and the "Book of the Chronicles of the Kings of Judah," which seem to be detailed annals or records kept by the kings that documented the events and reigns of Israel's and Judah's monarchs.

   For example:
   - **1 Kings 14:19**: "The rest of the acts of Jeroboam, how he warred and how he reigned, are written in the Book of the Chronicles of the Kings of Israel."
   - **1 Kings 15:7**: "The rest of the acts of Abijah and all that he did, are they not written in the Book of the Chronicles of the Kings of Judah?"

4. **Book of the Acts of Solomon:**
   - **1 Kings 11:41**: "Now the rest of the acts of Solomon, and all that he did, and his wisdom, are they not written in the Book of the Acts of Solomon?"

   This book is another lost document that apparently contained more detailed information about Solomon’s reign and his wisdom.

5. **Book of Enoch:**
   - **Jude 1:14-15**: "Enoch, the seventh from Adam, prophesied about them: ‘See, the Lord is coming with thousands upon thousands of his holy ones to judge everyone, and to convict all of them of all the ungodly acts they have committed in their ungodliness, and of all the defiant words ungodly sinners have spoken against him.’"

   Jude quotes from the Book of Enoch, a non-canonical Jewish work that has been preserved primarily in Ethiopian Orthodox traditions. The Book of Enoch includes prophecies, visions, and teachings attributed to Enoch.

6. **Epistle to the Laodiceans:**
   - **Colossians 4:16**: "After this letter has been read to you, see that it is also read in the church of the Laodiceans and that you in turn read the letter from Laodicea."

   This suggests there was an epistle to the Laodiceans, which is not included in the New Testament canon.

### Books like Maccabees and Jubilees

While there are no explicit mentions of books like **Maccabees** or **Jubilees** in the canonical texts of the Protestant Bible, these books are part of the **Apocrypha** and the **Pseudepigrapha**. 

- **1 and 2 Maccabees** are found in the Catholic and Orthodox Christian Bibles as part of the deuterocanonical books. They detail the history of the Maccabean Revolt against the Seleucid Empire and the Jewish struggle for religious freedom.

- **Jubilees** is considered a pseudepigraphal work (not included in most versions of the Bible). It is known as "The Little Genesis" and is often referenced in discussions about early Jewish interpretations of Genesis.

These books are referenced indirectly through their influence on Jewish thought and tradition during the Second Temple period and are part of the larger body of literature that includes other ancient texts.
